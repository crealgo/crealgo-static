Pick a process:
  1. 1
    Design Creation
    The first step in screen printing is to create the design that will be printed. This design is typically created using graphic design software and is saved as a high-resolution file.
  2. 2
    Screen Preparation
    Once the design is created, a mesh screen is prepared by coating it with a light-sensitive emulsion. The emulsion is then allowed to dry, and the design is printed onto a transparent film.
  3. 3
    Exposure
    The printed film is placed on the prepared screen and exposed to a bright light. The light hardens the emulsion on the screen, except for the areas where the design has been printed. These areas remain soft and can be washed away later.
  4. 4
    Washing
    After exposure, the screen is washed with water, which removes the soft emulsion, leaving the design on the screen.
  5. 5
    Ink Preparation
    The ink is mixed and prepared according to the color and type of substrate being printed on.
  6. 6
    Printing
    The substrate is placed on the printing press, and the screen is placed on top of it. Ink is then poured onto the screen and spread across it using a squeegee. The ink is forced through the mesh and onto the substrate, creating the printed design.
  7. 7
    Drying and Curing
    After printing, the substrate is dried and cured to ensure that the ink is fully set and will not smudge or wash away.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is screen printing?
Screen printing is a printing technique that involves using a mesh stencil (or "screen") to transfer ink onto a substrate, such as a t-shirt, poster, or sticker.
What types of materials can be screen printed?
Screen printing can be done on a wide variety of materials, including fabric, paper, metal, glass, and plastic.
What are the advantages of screen printing?
Screen printing is a versatile and cost-effective printing method that can produce high-quality, long-lasting prints. It can also be used to print on a wide range of materials and is suitable for both large and small production runs.
What is the process of screen printing?
The process of screen printing involves creating a stencil, or "screen" of the design to be printed, which is then placed over the substrate. Ink is then applied to the screen and forced through the mesh onto the substrate using a squeegee. This process can be repeated for multiple colors or layers.
What types of designs are suitable for screen printing?
Screen printing is best suited for designs with bold, solid colors or simple, graphic shapes. It is less suitable for designs with fine details or complex gradients.
What is the turnaround time for a screen printing order?
Turnaround times for screen printing orders can vary depending on the size of the order and the complexity of the design. Generally, orders can be completed within a few days to a few weeks.
Can I provide my own artwork for screen printing?
Yes, most screen printing companies will accept customer-provided artwork. However, it is important to ensure that the artwork meets certain technical specifications to ensure the best possible print quality.
